The Persistence of Gender Inequality

It is no secret that women's rights are currently at stake in America with the overturning of Roe v. Wade. Though much progress has been made for women's rights in the past century, the threat to reproductive rights, same-sex marriage, and to transgender healthcare are all too real. These policies disproportionately harm people of marginalized class, race, and gender identities.

With ever rising rates of hate crimes, homelessness, and poverty in these communities - it is clear that more than ever, changes need to be made to the systems that dictate this injustice. No one should have to leave their house with the fear of violence lingering in the back of their minds - but this is the reality for many people.

People of Significance

Marsha P. Johnson

Marsha P. Johnson was an important figure and activist for gay and transgender rights throughout the 1960's and 70's. She is well known for being on the front lines of the Stonewall Riots, and showing unconditional care and love for herself and her community.

Audre Lorde

Audre Lorde was a poet, author, and activist for womens and LGBT rights. She is well known for her poetry, much of which reflects her personal and raw experiences as a black lesbian woman. She was critical of exclusionary feminism. She sought to acknowledge differences among women in a way that encouraged solidarity rather than division.

Julia Serano

Julia Serano, is an author, biologist and performance artist. In her book "Whipping Girl" she writes about her experiences as a transgender woman, challenging peoples preconcieved beliefs and myths about trans women.
